This fixes two confirmed bugs found through real conversation testing, with side-by-side proof from an actual chat transcript. Read both sections fully before making changes, since they're related but require different fixes.

SECTION 1 — THE HIRING BRIEF SIDEBAR IS FILLING FIELDS WITH WRONG OR MISROUTED DATA, AND IS COPY-PASTING INSTEAD OF REASONING

Proof from a real conversation: the founder never mentioned seniority as "Junior" anywhere in the conversation, the discussion was entirely about a senior or staff-level engineer reporting to a CTO, yet the sidebar showed "SENIORITY: Junior." The founder's answer to "why are you hiring" (company grew from 30 to 120 customers) got placed into the TIMELINE field, while the founder's actual timeline answer ("1 month or less") was never used anywhere. The BUDGET field showed a truncated, meaningless fragment ("$2") despite no budget ever being discussed in that part of the conversation. The ROLE field showed a near-verbatim repeat of the company description rather than an actual role summary.

Find wherever in the codebase the sidebar's per-field data is generated and updated during the live conversation. The current implementation appears to be doing one of two broken things: either naively grabbing a snippet of the most recent user message and assigning it to whatever field is currently expected next, regardless of whether that message actually answered that field, or using unreliable keyword matching that misroutes answers to the wrong field entirely. Both of these explain the misrouting and truncation seen above.

Fix this properly: each field in the sidebar should be populated by having the AI genuinely interpret what's been established in the conversation so far and write a short, accurate, qualitative summary specific to that field, not a copy-pasted fragment of the user's literal message and not a guess based on message order or timing. If the founder's most recent message doesn't clearly answer the field currently being tracked, do not force something into that field, leave it in the "still gathering" state instead of filling it with the wrong content. This must use actual reasoning over the full conversation context, not positional or naive extraction.

After fixing this, run a full test conversation yourself and verify, field by field, that what appears in the sidebar genuinely matches what was actually said and means what it claims to mean: confirm seniority shown matches what was actually discussed, confirm timeline shows the actual stated timeline, confirm budget is only populated when a real budget was discussed, and confirm role is a real role summary, not a repeated company description.

SECTION 2 — THE AI IS STILL CLOSING THE CONVERSATION AFTER ONLY 3 TO 4 QUESTIONS, SKIPPING MOST REQUIRED FIELDS

Proof from the same conversation: only company context, role and responsibilities, seniority and reporting structure, and timeline and why-hiring were asked, then the AI moved straight to requesting contact information and closing. Tech stack, engagement type and duration, remote versus hybrid versus in-office, must-have versus nice-to-have technical skills, must-have versus nice-to-have culture fit, red flags or deal-breakers, and budget were never asked at all.

This means the required field checklist instruction in SYSTEM_PROMPT is still not being followed correctly, despite previous attempts to fix this. Before making any change, open chat.ts and read the entire current SYSTEM_PROMPT constant from top to bottom. Identify specifically why the model is treating four answered topics as sufficient to close: check whether the instruction to track missing required fields before closing is actually present and clearly stated as a hard requirement, or whether it's been weakened, removed, or contradicted by another instruction elsewhere in the prompt, which is a known recurring issue from previous edits in this project.

Rewrite the relevant section of SYSTEM_PROMPT, as a full clean replacement of that section rather than a partial patch, to make the closing condition explicit and mechanical rather than left to the model's judgment of when the conversation "feels" complete. State directly that the AI must internally track which of the following specific fields have been collected so far at every turn: company context, actual role and day to day responsibilities, new role or replacement, seniority and reporting structure, minimum years of experience, tech stack, must-have versus nice-to-have technical skills, must-have versus nice-to-have culture fit or soft skills, red flags or deal-breakers, engagement type and duration, remote versus hybrid versus in-office, start date or urgency, budget and additional compensation. The AI must not ask for contact information or close the conversation while any of these remain unaddressed, regardless of how naturally the conversation seems to be wrapping up.

After applying this, run a full test conversation yourself, and explicitly count how many distinct required topics were actually asked about before contact information was requested. Confirm it covers all of the fields listed above, not just four or five of them, before reporting this as fixed.
